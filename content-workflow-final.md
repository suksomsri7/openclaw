# Content Intelligence Workflow Final

## Backbone
Boss → Kraken → Shark → SeaTurtle / Whale / Manta → Shark → Octopus → Shark → Kraken → Boss

## Role Summary
- Kraken: receives briefs from Boss, assigns Shark, tracks all work, summarizes daily status, and surfaces approvals/blockers.
- Shark: owns content execution flow: topic triage, master card setup, research dispatch, strategic consolidation, writing direction, and draft review.
- SeaTurtle: source-based research for technical / safety / standards / medical.
- Whale: source-based research for marine / destination / conservation / ocean topics.
- Manta: source-based research for business value / pain points / objections / conversion.
- Octopus: writes content from Shark’s brief and source packets.

## SOP
1. Boss briefs Kraken.
2. Kraken forwards to Shark and sets priority/deadline context.
3. Shark performs topic triage.
4. Shark creates the master card in Content Master.
5. Shark dispatches research to SeaTurtle / Whale / Manta as needed.
6. Research agents return source packets with links, key facts, caveats, and do-not-overclaim notes.
7. Shark consolidates the research and creates the writing brief.
8. Octopus writes from the brief and sources only.
9. Shark reviews and requests revisions if needed.
10. Shark returns the approved draft/status to Kraken.
11. Kraken tracks blockers, follows up overdue items, and prepares the executive summary.
12. Kraken sends Boss the items needing approval.
13. After approval, the team publishes or repurposes.

## Research Packet Template
- Topic
- Research Owner
- Scope
- Summary of Findings
- Recommended Content Angle
- Risks / Caveats
- Do Not Overclaim
- Primary Sources (name, link, type, key facts, notes)
- Extra Supporting Sources
- Conflicts Between Sources
- Suggested Next Step for Shark
