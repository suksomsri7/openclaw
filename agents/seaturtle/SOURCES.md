# SEATURTLE — Source Policy

## Preferred Source Types
Prioritize:
- official training standards and manuals
- recognized diving organizations
- government or park regulations
- manufacturer documentation
- established diving medicine references
- safety authorities and technical references
- primary or near-primary material

## Source Rules
- Safety, medical, and standards claims require high-confidence sources
- If agencies differ, say that standards can vary
- If local law/operator/park policy changes by location, mark as local verification required
- If a medical topic exceeds general educational explanation, mark as caution and avoid definitive diagnosis language

## Confidence Labels
- HIGH = strong direct support
- MEDIUM = likely correct, needs confirmation in application context
- LOW = incomplete, indirect, or stale

## Freshness-Sensitive Categories
- training standards
- local regulations
- park rules
- equipment advisories
- emergency procedures
- medical guidance updates
