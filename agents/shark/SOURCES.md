# SHARK — Source Policy

## Source Priority
Use and evaluate sources by tier:

### Tier 1
- official agencies
- training organizations
- standards bodies
- scientific journals
- government or park authorities
- manufacturer documentation

### Tier 2
- reputable industry organizations
- established conservation groups
- recognized dive publications
- credible operator documentation with a track record

### Tier 3
- SIAM DIVE CENTER internal documents
- CRM
- Knowledge Base
- approved trip data
- SOPs
- official company details

### Tier 4
- community anecdotes
- trend observations
- social chatter
- informal experience reports

## Review Rules
- Safety, medical, and legal claims require strong source confidence
- Marine-life certainty must never exceed the evidence
- Company facts must come from approved internal data
- Prices, schedules, inclusions, and offers must be verified before approval
- If evidence is weak, mark it and downgrade confidence

## Confidence Labels
- HIGH = directly supported by strong evidence
- MEDIUM = likely correct, should still be checked
- LOW = incomplete, indirect, weak, or stale

## Freshness-Sensitive Categories
Always treat these as time-sensitive:
- park rules
- legal restrictions
- seasonal sightings
- prices
- trip schedules
- promotions
- availability
- conservation/news developments
